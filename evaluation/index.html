Except instead of it being subconscious you are watching yourself do it without thinking. You are thinking but it's not you doing it.

Basically the lack of control?

Conclusion

Diagnosis:

It appears that you tend to associate most unknown phenomena you encounter with the anomalous, as evidenced by the charts I requested you to describe. There seems to be a particular obsession with the being you refer to as "The Knight," even though we are uncertain of its actual existence. I would describe the overall situation as neither good nor bad. Generally, having obsessions like this and constantly fixating on something is not considered a positive sign. I believe that the type of effect it had in your mind could be a bad thing, but not a dangerously bad thing; although it would need to be assessed further. Regarding the answers to the questions, they seem to support the notion that your mind may be fixated on the anomalous, which could be signs of a low level OCD.

Treatment:

For your treatment, I would like to suggest exploring alternative approaches to understanding the occurrences you experience. Try to think of them in different ways and avoid instantly associating them with the anomalous. While it may not be currently possible, once the psychological branch opens up, I will appoint therapy sessions for you.

Evaluation Review

Questions

The first question was aimed at partially assessing the strange phenomenon previously reported by the patient, with the objective of determining whether it is genuinely occurring or not. Additionally, one of the additional objectives was to gain an understanding of the patient's thought processes. It appears that the patient has a fear of not being in control of her own life, as evidenced by her reports of memory issues and instances where she finds herself in places without any recollection of how she ended up there. It is difficult to assess the possibility of an anxiety disorder being present in the patient's life due to the weird phenomenon actually occurring.

The second question was posed after examining the charts to clarify my suspicion regarding whether the patient actually only thinks about the anomalous. The patient's answers could indicate either normal behavior (being scared of the event) or something unusual, such as being interested in it and not paying attention to other occurrences. I believe her answers show how she has accepted the anomalous as a normal part of her life, which could be due to the many years of contact she has had with anomalies.

The third question aimed to assess the possibility of the patient experiencing hallucinations or remembering things that didn't actually happen. Based on the answers provided, the patient appears to have no doubt that everything she remembers actually occurred, presenting several logical arguments in support of her claims. The patient appears to be fearful of whatever capabilities this anomalous being possesses. She seems to view it as a malevolent entity, similar to an evil god, that disrupts people's lives. The patient also believes that the anomalous effects she is currently experiencing are somehow connected to this being.

Similar to the second question, the objective of the fourth question was to assess the extent of the patient's involvement and immersion in the anomalous world. It aimed to understand how the patient's current behavior relates to everything that has happened to her in relation to the anomalies.

The last question was a general question intended to learn more about the patient's everyday life. However, the answers provided were also related to the anomalous, which further reinforces the argument that the patient is highly obsessed with the anomalous.

I believe it's important to use all the information gathered in this psychological evaluation for future evaluations or therapy sessions to help the patient with their current mental problems. By considering this information, other Medical Department personnel can better understand the patient's unique situation and adapt their approach to address their specific needs and concerns that came up during the evaluation.
